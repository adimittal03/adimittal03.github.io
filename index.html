Aditya Mittal


Hi! I am a fourth-year undergraduate student at UC Davis majoring in Statistics with minors in Mathematics and Computer Science.
My research focuses on developing novel algorithms that promote trustworthiness in machine learning and responsible AI systems. I am broadly interested in algorithmic fairness-related issuesâ€”particularly in quantifying and mitigating bias in predictive modeling applications. Ongoing projects include:

  • Improving Fairness in Predictions of Black-Box Machine Learning Models. We present TowerDebias, a novel post-processing technique designed to minimize bias by minimizing correlations between sensitive attributes and model predictions. Our results show TowerDebias effectively improves fairness with minimal performance loss, highlighting the fairness-accuracy trade-off.

  • Creating tools to improve quantitative literacy among statistics students, emphasizing fairness topics (q-FFL, TERM)

  • Leveraging GNNs and LLMs on text-attributed graphs to predict missing links in hardware security applications (AdaDPS, DP2)

I am fortunate to be advised by Dr. Norm Matloff for my undergraduate thesis, TowerDebias: A Novel Debiasing Method Based on the Tower Property.
